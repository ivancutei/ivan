MANILA: MODE OF TRANSPORTATION

JEEPNEY

Originated on:1941

“KING OF THE ROAD”

Jeepneys, Philippines' favored public transit, originated post-WWII using surplus American Willys Jeeps, repurposed with steel bodies, fabric covers, and benches for 20 passengers.

LRT

Originated on: 1985

"RIDE THE FUTURE"

The Manila Light Rail System (LRT) serves Metro Manila, initially light rail, now rapid transit, with 2 lines, 33.4 km, mainly elevated, 5operated by the government and LRMC

TRICYCLE

Originated on: 1970

"YOUR COMPACT RIDE"

Tricycles are a common mode of transport in the Philippines, with varying fares and payment methods. Know your estimated fare and potential extra charges for a hassle-free ride.

TAXI

Originated on: 1930

"YOUR DIRECT ROUTE"

Taxis are the Philippines' conventional transport, hailed on the street or pre-booked via websites or apps, providing a familiar, personalized travel option.

MOTORCYCLE TAXI

Originated on: 2019

"HABAL-HABAL"

A licensed motorcycle taxi, also called a cart bike or bike taxi, legally transports one passenger seated behind the motorcycle operator.
